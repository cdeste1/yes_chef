Agnolotti del Plin

Chef: Massimo Bottura
Source: adapted from Osteria Francescana (Modena, Italy)

An iconic dish from Piemonte, the pasta is filled with veal, pork, spinach, Parmigiano Reggiano, and eggs, then served in a sage butter sauce.

Ingredients

  • Stuffed Agnolotti (see sub-recipe)
  • 1 teaspoon Salt (plus more for pasta water)
  • 8–10 tablespoons Butter
  • 10 Sage leaves
  • 1 cup Parmigiano Reggiano DOP (grated)
  • 1 Fresh white truffle (optional)

Instructions

  1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Cook the agnolotti for 3–4 minutes, or until they float to the surface.
  2. Meanwhile, melt butter in a saucepan over medium heat. Add sage leaves and toast about 1 minute, then remove the leaves.
  3. Add 1 cup of pasta water to the butter, simmer for 2 minutes until reduced by half. Keep warm over low heat.
  4. Drain the agnolotti and toss them in the butter sauce. Cook 1 minute more over medium heat until the sauce bubbles. Remove from heat, add grated cheese, and serve immediately with shaved truffle if using.
