Blue Mussel Velouté with Saffron Foam & Compressed Sea Beans

A world-class main course inspired by Chef Yannick Alléno’s modern extraction philosophy. This dish features ultra-clean mussel essence, high-stability saffron foam, and vacuum-compressed sea beans for a multi-textural marine experience.

Serves 4

Ingredients:

  • 2kg – Live Blue Mussels (scrubbed and de-bearded)
  • 200ml – Dry White Wine (Chablis preferred)
  • 3 – Shallots (finely minced)
  • 1 – Bouquet Garni (thyme, bay leaf, parsley stems)
  • 500ml – Heavy Cream (minimum 40% fat)
  • 0.5g – Premium Persian Saffron (threads)
  • 2g – Soy Lecithin (for foam stability)
  • 1g – Xanthan Gum (for foam texture)
  • 100g – Fresh Sea Beans (Salicornia)
  • 2 – Finger Limes (citrus pearls)
  • 4 sheets – Edible Bronze Leaf (for garnish)

Steps:

  1. Sweat shallots in a heavy-bottomed pot without color. Increase heat, add mussels and wine, cover, and steam for exactly 3 minutes.
  2. Strain immediately, reserving all liquor. Deshell mussels and store in a small amount of liquid to maintain moisture.
  3. Pass the liquor through a fine-mesh chinois lined with a coffee filter. Reduce by half on an induction system.
  4. Add heavy cream to the reduction and simmer until the sauce coats a spoon. Hold at exactly 70°C using the Control Freak to maintain emulsion.
  5. For the foam: Combine 200ml reserved liquor, saffron, lecithin, and xanthan gum. Use the Sonicator for 90 seconds to extract color and create a permanent emulsion.
  6. Compress sea beans: Place sea beans and 1 tbsp liquor in a vacuum bag. Use the Chamber Vacuum Sealer on high to infuse flavor and alter texture.
  7. Plate: Arrange 5-7 mussels in the center of a warmed bowl. Pour the velouté around the base.
  8. Garnish with compressed sea beans and finger lime pearls.
  9. Finish by spooning the sonicated saffron foam over the top and adding a fragment of bronze leaf.
