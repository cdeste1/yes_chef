Cacao Sablé with Fleur de Sel Caramel
Source: adapted from: Classic French Bistro
Inspiring Chef: Alain Ducasse

A refined Parisian dessert combining a crisp cacao sablé base with a luscious fleur de sel caramel filling, a balance between bitterness, sweetness, and salt.

Serves 6

Ingredients:

  • Cacao sablé bases (see sub-recipe)
  • Fleur de sel caramel (see sub-recipe)
  • Optional – Chocolate shavings or gold leaf (for garnish)

Steps:

  1. 1.Place cooled cacao sablé rounds on a wire rack or plate.
  2. 2.Spoon or pipe a layer of warm caramel over each sablé.
  3. 3.Allow caramel to set slightly at room temperature.
  4. 4.Finish with a light sprinkle of fleur de sel and optional chocolate shavings or gold leaf.
  5. 5.Serve at room temperature to enjoy the textural contrast between crisp sablé and soft caramel.

Cacao Sablé Base
Yield: 6
Ingredients:
  • 120 g – Unsalted butter (softened)
  • 60 g – Powdered sugar
  • 150 g – All-purpose flour
  • 25 g – Cocoa powder (high-quality Dutch-processed)
  • 1 – Egg yolk
  • Pinch – Sea salt
Steps:
  1. 1.Cream butter and powdered sugar until smooth and pale.
  2. 2.Add egg yolk and mix to combine.
  3. 3.Sift flour, cocoa powder, and salt together; add to butter mixture.
  4. 4.Mix until dough comes together, then shape into a disk and chill 30 minutes.
  5. 5.Roll out to 4 mm thickness and cut into rounds using ring molds.
  6. 6.Bake at 170°C (340°F) for 10–12 minutes until firm. Cool completely.
Fleur de Sel Caramel
Ingredients:
  • 100 g – Granulated sugar
  • 20 g – Glucose or light corn syrup
  • 100 mL – Heavy cream (warmed)
  • 40 g – Unsalted butter (cubed)
  • 1 g – Fleur de sel (or to taste)
Steps:
  1. 1.In a saucepan, heat sugar and glucose until it melts and turns deep amber.
  2. 2.Carefully whisk in warm cream in stages — mixture will bubble vigorously.
  3. 3.Remove from heat and whisk in butter until smooth and glossy.
  4. 4.Add fleur de sel and stir to combine.
  5. 5.Cool slightly until thickened but still pourable.
