Black Truffle Brioche French Toast with Foie Gras Custard, Compressed Berries & Maple-Sauternes Espuma

A decadent fine-dining brunch composition featuring brioche soaked in a foie gras-enriched custard, caramelized in beurre noisette, paired with vacuum-compressed berries, maple-Sauternes espuma, and finished with fresh black truffle and textural frozen fruit shards.

Serves 4

Ingredients:

  • 8 slices – day-old brioche (high butter content, thick cut)
  • 200g – foie gras (veins removed)
  • 400ml – heavy cream
  • 4 – egg yolks
  • 20g – sugar
  • 5g – fine sea salt
  • 1g – white pepper
  • 10ml – cognac
  • 100g – clarified butter
  • 30g – brown butter (beurre noisette)
  • 1 cup – strawberries (halved)
  • 1 cup – raspberries
  • 50g – sugar (for berries)
  • 1 – orange (zest only)
  • 10ml – aged balsamic vinegar
  • 100ml – maple syrup (Grade A)
  • 50ml – Sauternes wine
  • 100ml – heavy cream (for espuma)
  • 2g – soy lecithin
  • 1 small – black truffle (fresh, for shaving)
  • to finish – edible flowers
  • to finish – flaky sea salt
  • optional – gold leaf

Steps:

  1. 1.Prepare foie gras custard: Gently heat foie gras with cream until just melted. Blend until completely smooth. Whisk in egg yolks, sugar, salt, white pepper, and cognac. Pass through a fine chinois. Vacuum seal and cook in a precision immersion circulator at 63°C (145°F) for 45 minutes. Cool slightly.
  2. 2.Prepare compressed berries: Toss strawberries and raspberries with sugar, orange zest, and aged balsamic. Vacuum seal in a chamber vacuum sealer on high pressure to compress and intensify flavor. Chill.
  3. 3.Prepare maple-Sauternes espuma: Warm maple syrup and Sauternes together. Add cream and soy lecithin, then blend. Transfer to a Gourmet Whip, charge with 1 N2O cartridge, and hold warm.
  4. 4.Soak brioche: Submerge brioche slices in warm foie gras custard for 10–15 minutes until fully saturated but intact.
  5. 5.Cook French toast: Heat clarified butter in a heavy pan to 180°C (356°F). Sear brioche until deeply golden on both sides. Baste with brown butter to finish.
  6. 6.Prepare frozen berry shards: Blend a portion of compressed berries into a smooth purée. Spread thinly onto an anti-griddle to instantly freeze, then break into irregular shards.
  7. 7.Plating: Place French toast off-center. Arrange compressed berries around. Dispense maple-Sauternes espuma in controlled quenelles. Insert frozen berry shards vertically for height. Finish with freshly shaved black truffle, edible flowers, flaky sea salt, and optional gold leaf.
