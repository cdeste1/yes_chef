Charred Calamari “Aegean Ember”
Source: Inspired by ABaC Barcelona
Inspiring Chef: Jordi Cruz

A Michelin-level calamari starter featuring binchotan-charred squid, smoked squid consommé, preserved lemon gel, black garlic emulsion, and squid ink rice lace crisp with dramatic fine-dining presentation.

Serves 4 tasting portions

Ingredients:

  • 2 large – fresh squid tubes (cleaned)
  • all from squid – squid tentacles
  • 1 tbsp – fine sea salt
  • 1 tsp – granulated sugar
  • 1 – lemon zest
  • 1 tbsp – olive oil
  • 40g – cultured butter
  • 1 tsp – squid ink
  • to finish – Maldon sea salt
  • as needed – bronze fennel fronds
  • as needed – micro shiso
  • as needed – finger lime pearls
  • as needed – edible flowers

Steps:

  1. Score the inside of the squid tubes in a crosshatch pattern without cutting through completely.
  2. Mix sea salt, sugar, and lemon zest. Lightly cure squid for 20 minutes in the refrigerator.
  3. Rinse squid gently and pat completely dry.
  4. Prepare smoked squid consommé
  5. Prepare preserved lemon gel
  6. Prepare black garlic emulsion
  7. Prepare squid ink rice lace crisp
  8. Brush squid lightly with olive oil and grill over extremely hot binchotan charcoal for 45–60 seconds per side until lightly blistered.
  9. Melt cultured butter with squid ink and glaze the grilled calamari immediately after cooking.
  10. Rest calamari for 1 minute before slicing into elegant ribbons.
  11. Plate smoked consommé reduction in the center of matte black stoneware.
  12. Arrange calamari ribbons and tentacles vertically for height and movement.
  13. Add dots of preserved lemon gel and swipes of black garlic emulsion.
  14. Insert rice lace crisp shards for texture and architecture.
  15. Finish with herb oil, finger lime pearls, bronze fennel, edible flowers, and Maldon salt.

Smoked Squid Consommé

Yield: 250ml

Ingredients:

  • reserved – squid trimmings
  • 1 – shallot (sliced)
  • 1 clove – garlic
  • 250ml – fish stock
  • 100ml – dry vermouth
  • 1 strip – kombu
  • 1 tsp – bonito flakes

Steps:

  1. Roast squid trimmings until deeply caramelized.
  2. Add shallot and garlic, then deglaze with vermouth.
  3. Add fish stock and kombu and simmer gently for 25 minutes.
  4. Add bonito flakes and steep for 3 minutes.
  5. Strain through a fine chinois.
  6. Cold smoke using a smoking gun under a cloche for 1 minute.

Preserved Lemon Gel

Yield: 1 squeeze bottle

Ingredients:

  • 2 tbsp – preserved lemon flesh
  • 100ml – water
  • 1 tsp – sugar
  • 1g – agar agar

Steps:

  1. Blend preserved lemon, water, and sugar until smooth.
  2. Whisk in agar agar and boil for 30 seconds.
  3. Set in a shallow tray until firm.
  4. Blend again until glossy and smooth, then transfer to squeeze bottle.

Black Garlic Emulsion

Yield: 1 cup

Ingredients:

  • 5 cloves – black garlic
  • 1 – egg yolk
  • 1 tsp – Dijon mustard
  • 120ml – grapeseed oil
  • 1 tsp – sherry vinegar

Steps:

  1. Blend egg yolk, Dijon mustard, and black garlic until smooth.
  2. Slowly stream in grapeseed oil until emulsified.
  3. Finish with sherry vinegar and season lightly.
  4. Pass through a fine sieve for an ultra-smooth finish.

Squid Ink Rice Lace Crisp

Yield: 8 crisp shards

Ingredients:

  • 80g – rice flour
  • 250ml – water
  • 1 tsp – squid ink
  • 1 tsp – toasted nori powder

Steps:

  1. Whisk rice flour, water, squid ink, and nori powder together until smooth.
  2. Pour a very thin layer into a lightly oiled nonstick pan over medium-high heat.
  3. Allow mixture to bubble aggressively until a lacy structure forms.
  4. Cook until crisp and carefully transfer to paper towel.
