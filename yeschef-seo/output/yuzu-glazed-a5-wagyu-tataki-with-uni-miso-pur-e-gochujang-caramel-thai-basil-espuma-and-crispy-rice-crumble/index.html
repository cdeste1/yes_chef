Yuzu-Glazed A5 Wagyu Tataki with Uni-Miso Purée, Gochujang Caramel, Thai Basil Espuma, and Crispy Rice Crumble

Chef:
Source:

An exquisite Michelin-star Asian fusion course featuring premium A5 Japanese Wagyu lightly torched and glazed with yuzu, paired with silky uni-miso purée, spicy-sweet gochujang caramel, airy Thai basil espuma, and textural crispy rice crumble.

Ingredients

  • 300 g A5 Japanese Wagyu ribeye or striploin (trimmed)
  • 2 Tbsp Fresh yuzu juice
  • 1 Tbsp Light Japanese soy sauce
  • 1 tsp Yuzu kosho paste
  • 100 g Fresh Hokkaido or Santa Barbara uni roe
  • 50 g White shiro miso
  • 1 Tbsp Dashi stock
  • 3 Tbsp Korean gochujang
  • 2 Tbsp Cane sugar
  • 200 ml Full-fat coconut milk
  • 50 ml Heavy cream
  • 1 large bunch Fresh Thai basil leaves
  • 100 g Cooked sushi rice (cooled)
  • for garnish Micro shiso leaves, edible gold leaf, yuzu zest, toasted black sesame seeds

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Uni-Miso Purée, Gochujang Caramel, Thai Basil Espuma, and Crispy Rice Crumble
  2. Season the A5 Wagyu lightly with sea salt. Vacuum-seal with yuzu juice, soy sauce, and yuzu kosho.
  3. Cook in sous-vide water bath at exactly 52°C (125.6°F) for 60 minutes.
  4. Shock in ice water for 10 minutes, pat completely dry, and reserve bag juices.
  5. Brush Wagyu with reserved juices and torch the surface evenly until deep mahogany crust forms (10–15 seconds per side).
  6. Slice against the grain into 5 mm thick pieces.
  7. Plating Layer 1: Place three precise dots of Uni-Miso Purée on the plate.
  8. Plating Layer 2: Arrange torched Wagyu slices in a gentle sweeping arc. Drizzle with warm Gochujang Caramel.
  9. Plating Layer 3: Dispense two elegant mounds of Thai Basil Espuma. Scatter Crispy Rice Crumble sparingly.
  10. Finish with micro shiso leaves, edible gold leaf flakes, microplaned yuzu zest, and toasted black sesame seeds.
