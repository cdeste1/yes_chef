Striploin with Fermented Black Garlic and Red Wine Jus
Source: adpated from CUT
Inspiring Chef: Wolfgang Puck

An indulgent fine dining dish featuring buttery Wagyu A5 striploin paired with the deep umami of fermented black garlic and a silky red wine reduction.

Serves 2–4

Ingredients:

  • Cooked Wagyu striploin (see sub-recipe)
  • Fermented black garlic purée (see sub-recipe)
  • Red wine jus (see sub-recipe)
  • for garnish – Micro greens or chive tips

Sub-Recipes:

Wagyu A5 Striploin
Ingredients:
  • 400–500 g – Wagyu A5 striploin (trimmed and portioned. A high quality striploin may be used instead of Wagyu.)
  • to taste – Sea salt
  • to taste – Freshly ground black pepper
  • 1 tbsp – Neutral oil (e.g., grapeseed)
  • 20 g – Butter
  • 1, crushed – Garlic clove
  • 1 sprig – Fresh thyme
Steps:
  1. 1.Bring the Wagyu to room temperature for 30–45 minutes before cooking.
  2. 2.Season lightly with salt and black pepper — the marbling provides richness, so avoid over-seasoning.
  3. 3.Melt butter, crushed carlic, and thyme over medium heat and keep warm
  4. 4.Heat neutral oil in a heavy pan over medium-high heat until shimmering.
  5. 5.Sear the striploin 45–60 seconds per side for medium-rare, basting with butter, garlic, and thyme during the final moments.
  6. 6.Rest the steak on a rack for 5–7 minutes before slicing.
Fermented Black Garlic Purée
Ingredients:
  • 6–8 – Fermented black garlic cloves
  • 1 tbsp – Olive oil
  • 1–2 tbsp – Water or light stock (for consistency)
  • Pinch – Salt (optional)
Steps:
  1. 1.Combine black garlic, olive oil, and a splash of water in a blender.
  2. 2.Blend until silky smooth, adding more liquid as needed for a purée consistency.
  3. 3.Taste and season lightly with salt if needed.
  4. 4.Pass through a fine sieve for an ultra-smooth texture.
Red Wine Jus
Ingredients:
  • 2 – Shallots (finely chopped)
  • 250 mL – Red wine (full-bodied, such as Cabernet or Bordeaux)
  • 200 mL – Beef or veal stock
  • 30 g – Butter (cold, cubed)
  • 1 tsp – Olive oil
  • 1 sprig – Fresh thyme
Steps:
  1. 1.Sauté shallots and thyme in olive oil over medium heat until soft and lightly caramelized.
  2. 2.Deglaze with red wine and reduce by two-thirds.
  3. 3.Add stock and continue to reduce until thick and glossy.
  4. 4.Strain through a fine sieve and whisk in cold butter to finish the sauce.
  5. 5.Adjust seasoning and keep warm.
