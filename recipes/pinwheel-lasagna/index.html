Pinwheel Lasagna
Source: adapted from: Don Angie - NYC
Inspiring Chef: Angie Rito & Scott Tachinelli

A rolled lasagna pinwheel filled with sausage bolognese, besciamella, mozzarella, and Parmigiano-Reggiano, baked in tomato sauce and finished with robiolina.

16 pinwheels (serves 8-10)

Ingredients:

  • Kosher salt (for the pasta water (see Note))
  • 8 sheets – Northern-Style Egg Yolk Pasta (12 × 7 inches, see sub-recipe; or use store-bought fresh pasta sheets)
  • 1 cup + 3 tablespoons – Besciamella (see sub-recipe)
  • 4 cups – Whole-milk mozzarella cheese (shredded)
  • 1 cup – Parmigiano-Reggiano cheese (finely grated)
  • 4 cups – Italian Sausage Bolognese (see sub-recipe)
  • 2 cups – 10-Minute San Marzano Tomato Sauce (see sub-recipe)
  • ½ cup – Robiolina cheese or whipped ricotta cheese
  • 2 tablespoons – Parsley (roughly chopped)
  • 3 tablespoons – Extra-virgin olive oil (for finishing)

Steps:

  1. 1.In a large pot, bring 4 quarts water and 1 cup kosher salt to a boil over high heat. Meanwhile, set up an ice bath and line a sheet pan with parchment.
  2. 2.Blanch each pasta sheet for 15 seconds, chill in the ice bath for 15 seconds, then dry on parchment. Layer sheets separated by parchment.
  3. 3.Lay one sheet out with the short side facing you. Spread ¼ cup besciamella, sprinkle 1 cup mozzarella and ¼ cup parmesan evenly.
  4. 4.Add another pasta sheet on top. Spread 1 cup Bolognese evenly, leaving 2-inch border. Add 2 tsp besciamella on top edge to seal.
  5. 5.Roll up from bottom into a log. Chill seam-side down for 1–2 hours to firm.
  6. 6.Repeat to form 4 rolls total. Wrap tightly in plastic wrap if storing (2 days refrigerated or 3 months frozen).
  7. 7.Preheat oven to 400°F (205°C). Slice each roll into 4 pinwheels (1¾ inches thick).
  8. 8.Spread tomato sauce in 9×13-inch baking dish. Arrange 16 pinwheels cut-side down, slightly staggered.
  9. 9.Bake 20–25 minutes until golden. Add dollops of robiolina, bake 2–3 minutes more. Top with parsley and olive oil.
  10. 10.Serve immediately. Refrigerate leftovers up to 4 days.

Sub-Recipes:

Lasagna Sheet
Yield: 8 sheets
Ingredients:
  • 4 cups – All-purpose or '00' flour
  • 16 – Large Egg Yolk
  • 2 – Large Egg
Steps:
  1. 1.Place all ingredients in a bowl and mix until it forms a bowl.
  2. 2.Wrap in plastic wrap and rest for at least 30 minutes.
  3. 3.Use a lasagana roller to make the sheets.
Besciamella
Yield: 2½ cups
Ingredients:
  • ½ cup (1 stick) – Unsalted butter (cubed)
  • 1 head – Garlic (halved horizontally)
  • ⅓ cup – Shallots (thinly sliced (about 1 medium))
  • 1 – Bay leaf
  • 1 – Thyme sprig
  • ¼ teaspoon – Black peppercorns
  • 1 teaspoon – Kosher salt
  • ½ cup – All-purpose or '00' flour
  • 2 cups – Cold whole milk
Steps:
  1. 1.Melt butter; add garlic, shallots, bay leaf, thyme, peppercorns, and salt. Cook 2 minutes until shallots are translucent.
  2. 2.Add flour, cook until golden, about 5 minutes. Stir in milk and cook 5 minutes until thickened. Strain.
  3. 3.Refrigerate until cool. Keeps 3 days refrigerated or 3 months frozen.
Italian Sausage Bolognese
Ingredients:
  • 2 – Yellow onions (coarsely chopped)
  • 4 – Garlic cloves (peeled)
  • 5 – Celery ribs (coarsely chopped)
  • 2 – Carrots (coarsely chopped)
  • 4 ounces – Pancetta (cut into 1-inch cubes)
  • 1 pound – Sweet Italian sausage (casings removed)
  • 1 pound – Ground veal (Ground beef can be used as a substitute)
  • 3 tablespoons – Kosher salt
  • 2 tablespoons – Extra-virgin olive oil
  • ½ cup – Tomato paste
  • 1 cup – White wine
  • 2 cans (28 oz each) – Whole tomatoes (San Marzano DOP)
  • 2 cups – Whole milk
  • 2 whole – Star anise
  • 2 teaspoons – Colatura or fish sauce (Optional, but adds flavor. Add more to taste)
  • 1 tablespoon – Sugar
  • 2 tablespoons – Fresh lemon juice
Steps:
  1. 1.Process onions, garlic, celery, and carrots until finely chopped; set aside. Process pancetta until finely chopped.
  2. 2.Mix sausage and veal with salt.
  3. 3.Brown meat in olive oil 15 minutes. Remove and set aside. Drain excess fat but keep browned bits.
  4. 4.Cook pancetta until browned, 8–10 minutes. Add vegetables; cook 10–15 minutes until soft and darkened.
  5. 5.Add tomato paste; cook 5 minutes until caramelized. Stir in wine; reduce 5 minutes.
  6. 6.Crush tomatoes by hand. Return meat to pot with milk, star anise, and tomatoes. Simmer 2 hours, stirring occasionally.
  7. 7.Remove star anise. Stir in colatura, sugar, and lemon juice to re-emulsify. Store up to 1 week refrigerated or 3 months frozen.
10-Minute San Marzano Tomato Sauce
Ingredients:
  • 2 cans (28 oz each) – Whole San Marzano tomatoes (DOP)
  • ½ cup – Extra-virgin olive oil
  • 8 – Garlic cloves (smashed)
  • 1 tablespoon + 1 teaspoon – Kosher salt
  • ¼ teaspoon – Crushed red pepper flakes
  • 1 tablespoon – Sugar
  • 2 – Basil sprigs
Steps:
  1. 1.Pulse tomatoes until broken but chunky.
  2. 2.Heat oil; add garlic and salt. Cook until golden, 2 minutes. Add pepper flakes and tomatoes. Bring to boil and remove from heat.
  3. 3.Add sugar and basil. Steep 5–30 minutes, then remove garlic and basil. Store up to 1 week refrigerated or 3 months frozen.
