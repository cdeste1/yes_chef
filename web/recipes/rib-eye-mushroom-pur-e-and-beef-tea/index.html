Rib-eye, mushroom purée and beef tea

Chef: Tom Keller
Source: adapted from The French Laundry (Yountville, USA)

An elegant course, pairing beef with mushroom purée and potatoes cooked with bacon.

Ingredients

  • 4 1/2 lb Rib-eye steak
  • 1 ounce Butter
  • 2 Garlic cloves (peeled and sliced)
  • 1/4 ounce Thyme Leaves
  • enough to rub the steak Extra-virgin olive oil
  • to season the steak salt and pepper
  • Mushroom Purée (see sub-recipe)
  • Beef Tea (see sub-recipe)
  • 3-4 Baby Carrots (a handful works)
  • 1 Little Gem Lettuce (substitute spinach or arugala)

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Beef Tea and Marcarie Potatoes
  2. Prepare the Mushroom Purée
  3. Blanch the baby carrots in boiling water for 3-5 minutes. Remove and put on ice to stop the cooking process
  4. Peel the skin off the carrots, add 3 1/2 ounces butter and 3 ounces water}
  5. Trim fat from the rib-eye.
  6. Trim the meat surrounding the rib-eye and set aside.
  7. Roll the trimmed meat in plastic wrap and cut into steaks.
  8. Place the rolled steaks, garlic cloves, and thyme leaves into a plastic bag and seal.
  9. Cook in a water bath at 117°F (47°C) for 4 hours.
  10. Remote from the bath, scrape off garlic and thyme and cut the braised beef in half. Store in the refrigerator.
  11. Season the rib-eye with salt and pepper.
  12. In a hot pan with olive oil, sear both side of the rib-eye
  13. Cut the braised beef into 1 inch pieces and add into the pan with 1 ounce of butter .
  14. When cooked to your liking, remove from pan and rest
  15. Peel the skin off the carrots and warm over low hear with 3 1/2 ounces butter and 3 ounces water
  16. Cut the Little Gem Lettuce
  17. Plating Layer 1: Place the Mushroom Purée on the plate. Place the braised beef pieces on the purée.
  18. Plating Layer 2: Place the Marcaire Potatoes on the plate. Cut the rib-eye into long slices and place on the potatoes.
  19. Plating Layer 3: Place the lettuce and carrots on the plate
