Squid Ink Tagliolini with Sea Urchin and Bottarga
Source: adapted from Le Bernardin (New York City, USA)
Inspiring Chef: Mauro Colagreco

Fresh squid ink tagliolini served with a rich sea urchin sauce, finished with grated bottarga and a drizzle of olive oil. A luxurious, delicate seafood pasta with bright lemon zest and optional herbs.

Serves 2

Ingredients:

  • Squid Ink Tagliolini (see sub-recipe)
  • Sea urchin sauce (see sub-recipe)
  • 1 – Lemon (for juice)
  • 20 g – Bottarga (mullet or tuna) (finely grated or shaved)
  • Remaining sea urchin roe (for garnish)
  • Extra virgin olive oil
  • Chives or micro basil (optional, for garnish)

Steps:

  1. 1.Cook pasta in heavily salted water for 2 minutes, until al dente.
  2. 2.Transfer directly to the sea urchin sauce with a splash of pasta water.
  3. 3.Toss gently until the pasta is glossy and coated. Adjust salt and acidity with lemon if needed.
  4. 4.Plate immediately, topping with remaining sea urchin roe, grated bottarga, a drizzle of olive oil, and optional herbs.

Sub-Recipes:

Squid Ink Tagliolini (Fresh Pasta)
Ingredients:
  • 200 g – "00" flour
  • 80 g – Semolina flour
  • 3 – Large eggs
  • 10 g – Squid ink (cuttlefish ink) (about 2 tsp)
  • 1 tsp – Olive oil
  • pinch – Salt
Steps:
  1. 1.Mix flours and salt in a bowl or on a clean counter.
  2. 2.Make a well, add eggs, squid ink, and olive oil. Beat with a fork, gradually incorporating flour until a dough forms.
  3. 3.Knead 10 minutes until smooth and elastic. Wrap in plastic and rest 30 minutes.
  4. 4.Roll out to 1 mm thickness using a pasta machine. Cut into tagliolini (~1/8 inch (2 mm) wide) using the spaghetti cutter. Dust lightly with semolina and set aside covered.
Sea Urchin Sauce
Ingredients:
  • 100 g – Fresh sea urchin roe (uni) (about 8–10 tongues)
  • 1 – Garlic clove (finely minced)
  • 2 tbsp – Unsalted butter
  • 2 tbsp – Extra virgin olive oil
  • 2 tbsp – Dry white wine
  • 1 small – Shallot (finely diced)
  • ½ – Lemon zest (from half a lemon)
  • Salt and black pepper (to taste)
  • 1 tbsp – Heavy cream (optional, for silkier sauce)
Steps:
  1. 1.Gently sauté shallot and garlic in olive oil until translucent; do not brown.
  2. 2.Add white wine and reduce by half.
  3. 3.Lower heat, whisk in butter until emulsified.
  4. 4.Add half of the sea urchin roe and mash gently to form a creamy sauce. Season with salt, pepper, and lemon zest. Keep warm on low heat.
