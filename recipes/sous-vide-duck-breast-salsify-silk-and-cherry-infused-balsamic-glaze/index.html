Sous-Vide Duck Breast, Salsify Silk, and Cherry-Infused Balsamic Glaze

A masterclass in precision, featuring ultra-tender duck with a glass-like crackling skin, served with an aerated salsify foam and a molecularly clarified cherry reduction.

2 plates

Ingredients:

  • 2 large – Moulard Duck Breasts (approx. 14-16oz each)
  • 1 lb – Fresh Salsify Roots (substitute parsnip if unavailable)
  • 1 cup – Fresh Sweet Cherries (pitted and halved)
  • 1/4 cup – Aged Balsamic Vinegar
  • 3 ounces – Unsalted European Butter
  • 1/2 cup – Heavy Cream
  • 1 – Lemon (for acidulated water bath)
  • 4 sprigs thyme, 2 cloves garlic – Aromatics (for the vacuum bag)
  • to finish – Maldon Sea Salt

Steps:

  1. 1.Score the duck skin in a 2mm cross-hatch pattern and season with salt and white pepper.
  2. 2.Place duck in a vacuum bag with thyme and garlic; use the Chamber Vacuum Sealer on 'High' to compress the meat.
  3. 3.Set Immersion Circulator to 135.5°F (57.5°C) and cook the duck for 90 minutes.
  4. 4.Prepare the Salsify Silk: Peel roots into acidulated lemon water, boil in salted water until soft, and purée with butter and cream.
  5. 5.Pass the purée through a Chinoise or Tamis twice to ensure zero grit.
  6. 6.Load the purée into the Whipping Siphon, charge with 2 N2O cartridges, and keep warm in a 50°C water bath.
  7. 7.Simmer cherries, balsamic, and sugar until soft; blend and strain through a fine mesh sieve to create the glaze.
  8. 8.Remove duck from bag and pat dry. Use the Infrared Thermometer to ensure a cold pan is at room temperature before starting.
  9. 9.Place duck skin-side down on low-medium heat for 12 minutes to render fat into a crispy crackling.
  10. 10.Flip and sear the meat side for 60 seconds; let the duck rest for 10 minutes before slicing.
  11. 11.Plating: Use the Siphon to create a cloud of Salsify Silk. Slice duck on a bias and fan out with tweezers.
  12. 12.Drizzle the cherry reduction and finish with micro-herbs and Maldon salt.
