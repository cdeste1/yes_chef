Sub-Recipes:

Burrata Sphere
Ingredients:
  • 7 ounces – Fresh burrata
  • 1/4 cup – Heavy cream
  • 1/2 teaspoon – Salt
  • 500 mL – Sodium alginate bath (1 g sodium alginate per 500 mL water)
  • 1 L – Calcium lactate bath (5 g calcium lactate per 1 L water)
Steps:
  1. 1.Open the burrata and remove the stracciatella filling.
  2. 2.Blend the stracciatella, cream, salt, and sodium alginate until completely smooth.
  3. 3.Chill 30 minutes to remove bubbles.
  4. 4.Using a round spoon or mold, form small portions and drop into the calcium lactate bath for about 2 minutes.
  5. 5.Rinse gently in cold water to remove any excess calcium.
  6. 6.Set aside in a little olive oil to prevent sticking.
  7. 7.Alternative: Skip steps 1-6 and use the burrata as is for a simpler presentation with similar flavor.
Basil Oil
Ingredients:
  • 1 large bunch – Fresh basil leaves
  • 200 mL – Neutral oil (grapeseed or light olive oil)
  • pinch – Salt
Steps:
  1. 1.Blanch basil leaves in boiling water for 10 seconds, then shock in ice water.
  2. 2.Squeeze dry, then blend with oil and salt until bright green.
  3. 3.Strain through a fine mesh sieve or cheesecloth.
  4. 4.Store refrigerated up to 5 days.
Tomato Water Snow
Ingredients:
  • 1 kg – Ripe tomatoes
  • to taste – Salt (about 1%)
  • pinch – Sugar (optional, if tomatoes are acidic)
  • 1/4 teaspoon – Xanthan gum (for thickening/emulsifying)
Steps:
  1. 1.Blend tomatoes with salt and sugar.
  2. 2.Line a bowl with cheesecloth, pour the purée in, and let it drip overnight in the fridge to collect tomato water.
  3. 3.Mix tomato water with Xanthan gum and freeze tomato water solid.
