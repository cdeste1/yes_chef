Pinwheel Lasagna

Chef: Angie Rito & Scott Tachinelli
Source: adapted from: Don Angie - NYC

A rolled lasagna pinwheel filled with sausage bolognese, besciamella, mozzarella, and Parmigiano-Reggiano, baked in tomato sauce and finished with robiolina.

Ingredients

  • Kosher salt (for the pasta water (see Note))
  • 8 sheets Northern-Style Egg Yolk Pasta (12 × 7 inches, see sub-recipe; or use store-bought fresh pasta sheets)
  • 1 cup + 3 tablespoons Besciamella (see sub-recipe)
  • 4 cups Whole-milk mozzarella cheese (shredded)
  • 1 cup Parmigiano-Reggiano cheese (finely grated)
  • 4 cups Italian Sausage Bolognese (see sub-recipe)
  • 2 cups 10-Minute San Marzano Tomato Sauce (see sub-recipe)
  • ½ cup Robiolina cheese or whipped ricotta cheese
  • 2 tablespoons Parsley (roughly chopped)
  • 3 tablespoons Extra-virgin olive oil (for finishing)

Instructions

  1. In a large pot, bring 4 quarts water and 1 cup kosher salt to a boil over high heat. Meanwhile, set up an ice bath and line a sheet pan with parchment.
  2. Blanch each pasta sheet for 15 seconds, chill in the ice bath for 15 seconds, then dry on parchment. Layer sheets separated by parchment.
  3. Lay one sheet out with the short side facing you. Spread ¼ cup besciamella, sprinkle 1 cup mozzarella and ¼ cup parmesan evenly.
  4. Add another pasta sheet on top. Spread 1 cup Bolognese evenly, leaving 2-inch border. Add 2 tsp besciamella on top edge to seal.
  5. Roll up from bottom into a log. Chill seam-side down for 1–2 hours to firm.
  6. Repeat to form 4 rolls total. Wrap tightly in plastic wrap if storing (2 days refrigerated or 3 months frozen).
  7. Preheat oven to 400°F (205°C). Slice each roll into 4 pinwheels (1¾ inches thick).
  8. Spread tomato sauce in 9×13-inch baking dish. Arrange 16 pinwheels cut-side down, slightly staggered.
  9. Bake 20–25 minutes until golden. Add dollops of robiolina, bake 2–3 minutes more. Top with parsley and olive oil.
  10. Serve immediately. Refrigerate leftovers up to 4 days.
